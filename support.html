Support Resources

By partnering with various organizations and providing comprehensive support packages, Here at Prevent we have successfully guided many individuals away from extremism and towards a positive path. However, if you feel uncomfortable speaking with us directly, there are several other trusted organizations you can turn to. Consider reaching out to a teacher, a local community organization, a faith leader, your local council, or a healthcare professional for guidance. Additionally, if you prefer to start by exploring resources online, there are numerous organizations available to offer advice and support. These resources can provide valuable information and help you find the assistance you need.

Educate Against Hate
Advice and trusted resources to help safeguard students from radicalisation, build resilience to all types of extremism and to promote our shared values.
NSPCC
Information, support and advice for anyone who is worried about a child.
Internet Matters
Protecting children from radicalisation and extremism. There's a chance that your child may meet people online or visit websites that could lead them to adopting extreme views or experiencing radicalisation.
Gov.uk
The aim of Prevent is to stop people from becoming terrorists or supporting terrorism. The objectives of Prevent are to: tackle the ideological causes of terrorism intervene early to support people susceptible to radicalisation enable people who have already engaged in terrorism to disengage and rehabilitate
