Micheal's story

Michael’s brother Thomas was an average teenager who enjoyed going to the pub and worked as an electrician. At 19, Thomas became interested in Islam and began to change. He started using his own pans for cooking and became angry when his family watched TV or listened to music. Thomas changed his name and appearance and lost his apprenticeship due to expressing extremist views at work. He traveled abroad, claiming he was going to study Arabic, but later called to say he had joined a terrorist group. Shortly after, Thomas was killed, leaving his mother and brother devastated. The video above is the heartbreaking letter Michael wished he could have written to Thomas.

John's Story

John, often in trouble at school and feeling left behind, attended a right-wing group meeting with a friend. He soon got involved in extremist activities online and at rallies. After inviting a teacher to a rally, his college referred him to the Prevent programme. A specialist mentor helped him gain confidence and realize the need for change, guiding him away from extremism.

Mustafa's story
